Common questions

Straight answers.

What hardware do I need?
A digital display you already own, a USB camera (any standard webcam works), and a small local PC — we recommend a Raspberry Pi 5 or mini PC, available for under £80. That's it. We provide the software.
What's the revenue split?
Operators keep the majority of CPM revenue. Adgrid takes a platform fee per verified impression delivered. Exact rates are being finalised with our early partner cohort — early operators influence the terms.
Is this legal to run in my venue?
Yes. Our system is GDPR-compliant by design — it counts and classifies people but does not identify or store individual faces. No biometric data is collected. We recommend a short notice to visitors (we provide a template), consistent with standard CCTV practice.
What if no advertisers target my area?
Your screen only displays campaigns when matched. If nothing matches, your screen continues as normal — we don't force filler ads. As the network grows, coverage improves. Early operators are prioritised for incoming campaigns.
What if the camera or connection goes offline?
Impression tracking pauses — no false data is ever reported. Advertisers are only charged for verified impressions. Your screen continues to display approved campaigns; the verification layer simply resumes when connectivity restores.
How many screens are live and where?
We're in early access — actively onboarding our first screen operators across the UK. Coverage details are shared during the advertiser onboarding call so you can confirm the network suits your campaign before committing.
How is impression data verified?
Computer vision runs on hardware at each screen location. When your ad plays, the system counts people in frame, records dwell time and attention metrics, and generates a tamper-evident certificate. No modelling, no extrapolation.
What ad formats do you support?
Static images and video. Resolution requirements and aspect ratios depend on the operator's screen — confirmed at campaign setup. We support standard DOOH formats.
Is the audience data GDPR-compliant?
Yes. Demographic data (age bracket, gender) is processed on-device and never transmitted as personal data. Advertisers receive aggregated counts only — no individual-level data, no retargeting IDs. Compliant with UK GDPR and ICO guidance.
What's the minimum spend?
Minimum spend is being finalised with our first advertiser cohort. Early advertisers influence pricing and get priority access to the best-performing screens. Apply to join the waitlist to discuss.
