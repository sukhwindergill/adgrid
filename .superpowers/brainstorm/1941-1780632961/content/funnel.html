Adgrid — Conversion funnel

Every section has one job. Each one earns permission to show the next.

Phase 1 — Hook (above the fold)
1
NAV — orientation
Sticky nav — minimal
Logo + two anchor links ("For Operators" / "For Advertisers") + Sign In. No pricing link. Visitor needs to know where they are and that there are two paths. That's it.
2
HERO — make the bold claim
⚡ The hook — state the problem the industry has
First 3 seconds. Don't explain the product — name the problem everyone in the industry already feels. Advertisers know CPM estimates are garbage. Operators know they're leaving money on the table. Lead with that pain.
"The ad industry sells you estimates. We sell you proof."

One sentence what we do. Two buttons: "I have screens" / "I want to advertise" — scroll to the right section. Nothing else above the fold.
Phase 2 — Belief (why this is real)
3
PROOF — show before you explain
📊 Live data card — "this is what verified looks like"
Before you explain how it works, show what the output looks like. The mock dashboard card (people in view, dwell time, attention score, demographics) does more selling than any paragraph. Visitors go "oh, I get it" before you've explained a thing.
"This is what your screen is delivering right now — not an estimate, not a survey. A camera counted it."

Keep the live-data card mockup. Add: "Verified impression certificate generated." That's the money line.
4
CREDIBILITY — why believe us
🔒 Privacy + tech credibility strip
Right after the data card, the smart visitor asks "wait — is this legal? Who sees this data?" Answer it immediately before they bounce. Short, confident, no legalese.
"All processing happens on your hardware. No images leave the device. Ever. Our system counts — it does not identify. GDPR-compliant by design."

3 sentences max. Link to full privacy page.
Phase 3 — Operator funnel (supply side first)
5
OPERATOR — how simple is this
🖥 For Operators — how it works (4 steps)
Operators are the supply side — you need them first, and they have the most friction (hardware). Make setup feel trivially easy. Lead with the outcome (earn money from screens you already have), then reveal the steps are simple.
"You already have a screen. Add a £60 camera kit. We handle the rest."

Steps: Register your screen → Camera connects locally → Approve campaigns you want → Stripe pays you automatically.

Key: say "you approve every campaign" — control anxiety is the #1 operator objection.
6
OPERATOR — what do I actually earn
💷 Revenue + control section
The "how it works" section explains the mechanics. This section sells the outcome. Answer: how much, how often, what's the split, do I lose control of my screen.
"Verified impressions command premium CPM. You keep the majority — we take a platform fee per verified impression delivered. Stripe payouts weekly. No lock-in."

Even without exact numbers, say "majority" and "weekly" — these signal fairness and reliability.
Phase 4 — Advertiser funnel (demand side)
7
ADVERTISER — what do I get
📢 For Advertisers — buy with confidence
Advertisers have one fear: "am I paying for real eyeballs?" Lead with the answer. Then explain targeting, formats, and the approval process (quality screens only).
"Every impression Adgrid sells comes with a verified count. Not modelled, not estimated — counted by a camera at the moment your ad played."

Then: target by location / time of day / demographic. Formats: static + video. Approval-gated screens = quality control. CTA: "Apply to advertise →"
Phase 5 — Objection kill + convert
8
FAQ — kill remaining objections
❓ FAQ — tabbed by audience
Anyone still reading at this point is interested but has a specific blocker. The FAQ is a closing tool, not an information dump. Only include questions that actually kill conversions if unanswered.
Operators tab: What hardware do I need? What's the revenue split? Is this legal in my venue? What if no advertisers target my area? What if the camera breaks?

Advertisers tab: How many screens are live? What locations? How is data verified? What formats? Min spend? GDPR compliant?
9
CTA — the ask
✉ Early access — email capture
Only one ask. No pricing. Reduce commitment to just an email. The dropdown tells you which audience they are so you can segment follow-up.
"We're onboarding our first operators and advertisers now. Early partners set the terms."

Email field + "I am a: [Screen operator / Advertiser / Both]" dropdown. One button: "Request access". No fake urgency. No countdown.
10
FOOTER
Footer — legal + sign in
Privacy · Terms · Contact · Sign In. All links must work. That's it.
The funnel logic in one sentence
Hook them with the industry problem → Show the proof before explaining it → Reassure on privacy → Walk operators through the easy path → Walk advertisers through what they buy → Kill remaining objections in FAQ → Ask for one low-commitment action (email).
