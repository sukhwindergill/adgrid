One Login — Dual Role: Which approach?

Screen owners who also want to advertise. Pick the model that feels right.

A

Sidebar Role Switcher

A small toggle at the top of the sidebar — "Screen Owner" / "Advertiser" — that swaps the entire nav + views. Same single-page app, same session. Think of it like switching workspaces.

Pros

  • Clean separation of context
  • Familiar pattern (Slack workspaces, Notion teams)
  • Easy to build on existing code

Cons

  • Context switch feels heavy if you hop between roles often
B

Unified Dashboard with Both Sections

One sidebar with all sections — Screens, Approval Queue (owner stuff) and Campaigns, Create Ad (advertiser stuff) — visible together. Role is additive, not a switch.

Pros

  • No switching needed — everything at once
  • Good for power users who context-switch constantly

Cons

  • Sidebar gets cluttered for new users
  • Mental model confusion — "am I looking at my screen or my ad?"
C

Role at Signup → Add Role Later

Advertiser or Screen Owner at signup. Later, from Settings, you can "add" the other role to your account. Once both roles are active, a header dropdown lets you switch. Role-specific nav loads per selection.

Pros

  • Simple for new users (one role at a time)
  • Opt-in — only power users see the switcher

Cons

  • More DB/profile changes needed
  • Onboarding for "add role" flow needs designing
